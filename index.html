What is Incision

INCISION is a medical term which describe the delibrate cutting of a tissue or organ for the purpose of diagonosis , prevention and treatment.

In other words it refers to a cut made into something , often reffering to a surgical cut made in the skin or flesh during an operation.

Types Of Incision

The type you choose depend on the location, patient preferences , availability of materials , type of condition etc.

Horizontal or transverse incision:

This is a horizontal incision across the body location suitable for this type is around abdominal or the thoracic region.

Vertical or midline incision:

A cut is made through the midline or central area of the body around the abdomen or the thoracic area . it is a vertical cut on the body .

Paramedian Incision:

As the name implies it is the type of incision made parallel to the midline.

Pfannestial Incision:

IT is common in caesarean section session or urologic surgeries often done above the pubic area.

Oblique Incision:

This is a slant cut like an 45degree angle . it is commonly used for appendencitis or gall bladder removals.

Others may include Y shape incision ,T-shape, Curvilinear incision , laparoscopic incision , Z-shape incision often used for reconstruction purposes.

Determinants for Incision

  • Depth of abscess or wound
  • Type of infection
  • Length of the tumour or infection
  • Location in the body cavity
  • Edges either smooth or rough
  • Level of bleeding
  • Response to treatment
  • Complication risk

Reasons for Incision

  1. To allow for the escape of the pus and dead cells
  2. To terminate multiplications of pathogenic agent
  3. To provide access to internal organs
  4. To repair damaged structures
  5. To remove diseased or damaged tissue
  6. To transplant organs
  7. To explore internal structures especially when non-invasive
